Macro Signal

Reserve scarcity forces banks to compete for settlement balances, but when aggregate supply tightens, the Fed becomes the only marginal supplier. This collapses what looks like a distributed market into a single-node dependency—liquidity provision centralises precisely when coordination demands peak.

The FEDS paper maps this threshold: where decentralised reserve management flips to Fed window reliance or emergency facilities. Watch the spread between SOFR and IORB during month-end turns. If it blows out past 15bp while reserves stay above $3 trillion, the system is already routing through the centre despite adequate headline liquidity. That's your fragility signal—coordination fails before absolute scarcity hits.

FEDS Paper: A Tale of Demand and Supply for Central Bank Reserves
FRB: Working Papers · 
Fed researchers isolate the interbank segment of fed funds to show non-bank lenders (FHLBs) supply reserves elastically while banks become price-inelastic as their own reserve balances fall. This explains why administered rates work in ample reserves but why transmission degrades as QT proceeds—the marginal pricing mechanism shifts from elastic non-bank supply to rigid bank hoarding behavior. The paper exposes the microstructure tension Lyn Alden references when discussing reserve scarcity thresholds: it's not a single number but a regime change in how different counterparties respond to rate signals. Malcolm should watch: if FHLB volumes spike while bank-to-bank dries up, reserves are functionally scarce even if headline RRP remains elevated.
If bank lenders continue showing price inelasticity, fed funds effective rate will decouple upward from IORB before official reserve scarcity is declared. Watch FHLB lending volumes vs bank-to-bank fed funds as the canary—inelasticity there precedes repo volatility.

Speculative decoding just shifted from academic toy to production-ready multiplier on real frontier models, changing AI inference cost structure in real time.
